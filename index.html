"What did you learn about the design process from your experience in this course?"

Write your first-person reflection here. Speak specifically — reference moments from your capstone, not textbook theory. What surprised you about iterative design? What assumption did the process force you to abandon?

Aim for genuine insight. The reader wants to know how your thinking evolved, not a summary of design steps they already know.

02

"What are the most important attributes of an engineer?"

Ground your answer in lived experience — not what a course outline says. What attribute, when absent, hurt your team? When present, made the difference? What did you observe in yourself that you didn't expect?

03

"How has this project contributed to your understanding of the professional engineer's role in society?"

Think beyond technical performance. What ethical, environmental, or accessibility considerations arose during your project? What responsibilities did you feel? How has this changed how you'll approach future work as an engineer?

04

"What strengths do you bring, and where do you need to grow?"

Be specific and honest. Strengths aren't only technical — communication, persistence, and creative problem-solving all count. For growth areas, go beyond naming them: describe concretely how you will develop each one.

End with a forward-looking sentence that captures your trajectory as an engineer stepping into the profession.
